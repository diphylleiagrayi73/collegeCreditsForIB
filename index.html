IB College Credit Guide

Welcome to IBCCG!

IBCCG was created as a CAS project by Giovanni Hernandez-Rios, Caroline Labb, Maggie O'Donnell, Zach Sahl & Arya Srivastava, a group of high school students participating in the International Baccalaureate (IB) program. During our college applications, we found it difficult to find which schools offer IB credit, and what specific criteria are needed to earn it. We designed this website as a tool for future applicants to find, learn about, and/or investigate schools that offer credit for IB courses.

What is IB credit?

After completing the IB program, students are awarded a score of 1-7 for each course they completed. Many institutions will accept those courses as credits towards achieving a degree for students who attain a certain grade. However, the credit boundaries depend on the school. A variety of factors such as the score needed to earn the credit, how many credits a course is awarded, what courses are accepted, and more can all contribute to the overall value of how much an IB credit is worth at a school. These credits can be greatly beneficial, as this can save you a great deal of time and money, as students have received up to a full year's worth of credit.
